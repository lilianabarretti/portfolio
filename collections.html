MARATUS VOLANS


Spring Fashion Collection 2017 by Liliana Barretti

Watch the video of the fashion show. I'm at 1:17




My collection is inspired by the beautiful peacock spider. Newly discovered and native to Australia, these tiny jumping spiders create the most elaborate visual displays in the animal kingdom. From fiery orange to splashy turquoise to glowing beige, this collection takes the vibrant colors and essence of the rainforest to the glitz and glamour of high-end culture. The asymmetrical lines, glistening crystals and acoustics of feathers definitely make a statement. The males peacock spiders are notable for their disco-like dance rituals, using their eight legs and bright, iridescent rumps to attract females. The ideal wearers are spontaneous and full of energy, live an eccentric life of adventure and spontaneity, and know their power and ability to reach their fullest potential.

Flash

Design concept & Purpose

This fashion-foward collection piece radiates a positive flash of energy, reflecting in every direction. It’s uplifting neon colors in the Spring ’16 color palette consists of fiesta orange, fuchsia pink and purple maroon. The maroon center line creates symmetry, as the preceding vertical lines turn diagonally towards the sides in order to follow the lines of the cold-shoulder top. The diagonal lines resemble the plethora directions she will go. The vertical lines resemble her clear path in her understanding of who she is and why she was put on this Earth. The top purposely is lifted above the mesh to create a unique design. It is top is modest in the front, while the cold-shoulder and the mesh wrapped around underneath makes it a bit more sexy. The choker accessory adds length and height while the wristband brings colors below the waistline. The long sheer skirt flatters the natural curves of the waist and is emphasized with sheer ruffle detailing. This collection top and skirt inspire others to reach for the stars in all of their endeavors and to keep powering through, because with determination and a strong personality, one will up where they are meant to.

Techniques & materials

I created this design with a delicate, stretchy mesh fabric with white polyester on top. The white top is one piece where I added sleeves by attaching the edge to the inside. The neon yarn serves as embroidery and detailing, which is hand-stitched with clear thread. I used sheer fabric with merrowed edges for the organic shaped detailing on the skirt. The garments are both a size 6.










Shine

Design concept & Purpose

This collection pieces is a white jumper, most similar to the men’s piece with its replicated shapes and spontaneous embroidery. The jumper has a mesh triangle at the back to make it edgy and less modest. It brings attention to the back of the garment as well as the plunging neckline at the front. The front purposely crosses and can be adjusted in how open or closed the wearer wants it, which gives the garment versatility in being worn more modest in the day versus more scandalous at night. The embroidery is spontaneous in some places and straight in others, resembling the sharp edges of sunlight’s gleam and the sun’s ability to light up darkness. This jumper embodies nothing but good vibes and indicates a bright future full of adventure and spontaneity.

Techniques & materials

I created this design using white woven polyester. The front was cut-on fold and I draped the rest of the top half. I made the pant legs patterns using CAD software for precise measurements and accuracy. I used a white waistband elastic to make the sizing versatile and easy to throw on and get the day started. The neon embroidery yarn was kept together to keep its thickness and boldness. This collection piece is a size 8 on a mannequin.










Power

Design concept & Purpose

This collection piece is very sophisticated men’s wear with its white, clean lines and both, geometric and organic shapes. This symmetrical design is a suit blazer with a stand up lapels to add some three-dimension. The neon orange embroidery is hand-stitched in an ecstatic pattern to display its high energy and activeness. The mesh is triangular and placed at the bottom of the sleeves and at top of the back to make the set edgy as well. The mesh is outlined with the neon yarn to add more boldness, power and symmetry. The straight pants are a sophisticated, professional white. This two-piece set makes for a ladies man because of its ability to attract and catch the eye, and convey the wearer’s consistent determination to succeed in his desired career.

Techniques & materials

I created this design with block patterns and CAD software, as well as draping on a size 38 male mannequin. I added the mesh to the sleeves and back then sewed the white fly zipper into the pants. The orange yarn was stitched to detail the stiff, white polyester fabric.










Aura

Design concept & Purpose

This collection turns the intangible aura, tangible with its mesh base and unique embroidery. An aura is a an energy field surrounding a person or object. It emanates a distinctive atmosphere of good qualities and good vibes. The wearer of the dress embodies positivity and gratitude. Its spontaneity is expressed in its embroidery and unique neckline. The criss-crossing lines keep the eye moving in every direction. The vertical lines on the dress resemble the ups and downs she undergoes in her relentless journey to reach her ideal destiny, with the bright colors resembling her constant positive outlook all situations. The curves at the top and bottom that connect each line resemble the roundness of a halo. The ruffled, sheer hem adds playfulness and spontaneity to the piece. This dress lights up the world around it and improves the emotion all those in its presence.

Techniques & materials

I made this dress out of a delicate mesh fabric. I superglued the neon yarn in place before using a clear thread to hand-stitch the embroidery details. Rather than your typical dress with two streps, this dress has several straps connecting the front to the back. Two pieces gracefully cross the front of the neck to resemble a choker, which is a popular accessory for spring. The yarn is thick and thin in certain spots to add more interesting detail and uniqueness. I used sheer fabric with merrowed edges to line the hem. The dress is a size 6.










Glow

Design concept & Purpose

This collection piece is a white, silky jumper that reflects subtle, luminous light. From the base to the embroidery, this jumper has a unique style and a memorable glow. This jumper is perfect for day or night and attending high-end events. She who embodies this piece has a natural, glow to her that is down-to-earth, humble and contagious. Her aura vibrates gratitude and achievement. This jumper influences the power of thought and inspires others to use the law of attraction to manifest good things into their life and to create their ideal future lifestyle. The embroidery all around this piece gives it a universal glow that sparks brings new ideas, new experiences and new actions. Its orange and fuchsia colors protrudes confidence and positivity.

Techniques & materials

I created this design by draping and using block patterns mostly for the pants. The sheer ruffles and neon yarn add flare and continuity throughout my collection. The neckline and armholes are plunging. There is a tight elastic waistband to flatter the natural shape of the waist and hips. This jumper is size 8 on a mannequin.
