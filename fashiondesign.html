American Beauty 2015

Challenge Statement:

Sketch, design, sew & show a red dress for the Annual Women's Heart Care Forum to raise awareness for heart disease.

The pantone color of my dress for the Red Dress Collection is called American Beauty. While most things in life are beautiful and glamorous, I want to raise awareness that heart disease is the leading killer amongst women. Through my design, I emphasize the power of the heart, the power of women, and the power to live the life every women deserves. My biggest statement is the heart beat hemline on the cocktail dress that resembles not only the high and low points in life that make us stronger, but also the power of the heart to beat 100,000 times per day. The electrifying, blue crystal embroidery on the long, red chiffon skirt resembles the blood flowing through 60,000 miles of blood vessels everyday. The embroidery is blue just like blood is blue before it hits oxygen and is exposed to reality. The heart is so powerful and full of energy that it has the ability to beat even if separated from the body because it has its own electrical pulse. My dress portrays a powerful, classy and sophisticated woman with a positive mindset and ability to achieve anything she sets her heart on.

Sheer Elegance

Challenge Statement

Create a transformable garment.

My goal was to try to and make the garment transform in multiple ways, so I decided to create a high-end looking dress that would be for the runway, red carpet or high-class events that would have a detachable and reattachable accessory. The ruffles can detach and be placed from the shoulder to the ankle for a unique silhouette and style. They can be placed around the neck as a ruffled scarf, around the waist to accentuate the hips and serve as some sort of a peplum, or act as a ruffled hem on the bottom. The ruffles can also be worn as an accessory to another outfit, or even a scarf in the fall or winter. This is practical because a lot of dresses cannot be worn more than once due to pictures, old styles and the idea of “newness.”

Process Photos

Materials: 2 yards tan gold party satin for the full gown and sleeves, tan lining fabric for the top portion, a 22-24in. beige invisible zipper, 4 yards Tulle-like material that in iridescent white and tan, embellishments and velcro to attach and detach the ruffles.



Exotic Reflection

Challenge Statement

Make a garment without fabric using unconventional materials.

My goal was to use my materials in ways that you would never think to see them. White rubber mats I walk on everyday are now being worn as garments, CD’s full of memory and music are now broken into pieces, and pantyhose are now structured with wire and hardened with white paint. As for my personal style in dress, I often wear bright, flashy colors, big statement jewelry, LED light up jackets and shoes, and apparel full of sparkles and glitter. In my design, I cut the white rubber mats in creative ways to create an edgy, sophisticated look to correspond with the rest of the dress and the CD’s sharp edges. The front has a plunging neckline because I wanted it to be scandalous with a rebellious feel. The top is very symmetrical in the front, with an uneven, yet symmetrical hemline on both sides to keep the edgy feel. The shoulder cuffs are made subtle by covering the metallic silver foil wrap with the same material as the rest of the white garment. This is to keep the main focus on the skirt. While much is happening in the front, I wanted to add a little Alexander McQueen flare by adding something unique and different to the back of the top. I vertically aligned the three irregular, white painted structures I handmade and put them in ascending order of size, top to bottom, as the backbone of the dress. The bottom half of my design is coved in broken CD pieces. The pieces reflect bright, holographic colors at different angles. The skirt extends with semi-sheer rubber strips conjoined aesthetically by white, vertical lines of duct tape. The uneven hemline of the skirt is purposely done to exaggerate the exotic, edgy feel and work synonymously with the top hemline. Overall my dress takes viewers on an exotic journey of bright colors, strong texture, reflections, sharp edges and abstract structures in a sophisticated, edgy way.







Process Photos

Materials

I used music CD's collected from home, knee-high stocking panty hose, white paint, glue, and bent wire to DIY the structures, and silver metallic foil sheets.

Prismatic Sweater

Challenge Statement

Create a transformable garment with the use of technology, such as LED lights, nitinol wires, controls, etc..

I made half-zip sweater that is wearable technology. The collar has nitinol shape alloy wire that is intertwined in a unique 3-D structure the iridescent fabric is shaped into. This allows the collar to expand and contract– giving it a variety of uses. It looks very elegant and high-class with a purpose. Whether the user is someone who is handicap or the user just has a handful of things or is doing activities such as exercising or skiing, the user just has to push the button on the left or right sleeve to activate the nitinol wire. The wire then convert electrical energy into mechanical energy by contracting the collar in closer to the neck while minimizing bulk and increasing style. It also will tighten the arm cuffs and the hem of the sweater to better trap in body heat to increase warmth and comfort.
